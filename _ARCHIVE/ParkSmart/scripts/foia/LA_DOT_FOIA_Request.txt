SUBJECT: Public Records Request – Parking Meter Locations and Street Parking Rules

This is a request under California Public Records Act (CPRA) for the following public records:

1. PARKING METER INVENTORY & RATES
   Complete dataset of all Los Angeles parking meters maintained by LADOT:
   - Meter ID / asset ID
   - Street address (address number, street name)
   - Council district or zone
   - Latitude and longitude coordinates
   - Hourly rate(s) (e.g., $0.50 - $2.50/hr, time-of-day variation)
   - Operating hours (e.g., 8 AM - 10 PM, days of week)
   - Maximum stay duration
   - Payment methods accepted
   - Effective date of current rates/rules

   Requested format: CSV, Excel (.xlsx), JSON, or GIS shapefile

2. STREET PARKING REGULATIONS (Curb Space Rules)
   Complete street-by-street parking regulation database:
   - Street name
   - From cross street / to cross street
   - Side of street (east, west, north, south)
   - Regulation type (NO PARKING, METER, STREET CLEANING, etc.)
   - Full rule text with times/days (e.g., "No Parking 7 AM - 10 AM, Mon-Fri")
   - Max stay limit (if applicable)
   - Permit zone (if applicable, e.g., "Resident Preferential Parking Zone A")
   - Effective date

   Requested format: CSV, Excel, JSON, or GIS format

3. STREET SWEEPING / STREET CLEANING SCHEDULE
   Complete street cleaning schedule:
   - Street name (or street segment)
   - Cleaning day(s) of week (e.g., Tuesday, Thursday)
   - Cleaning time windows (e.g., 7 AM - 12 PM)
   - Council district
   - Effective schedule date

   Requested format: CSV, Excel, JSON, or text table

4. PARKING LOT DATA (Public Lots)
   If available, LADOT-maintained public parking lot information:
   - Lot address
   - Total spaces / capacity
   - Rate structure (hourly, daily, flat)
   - Hours of operation
   - Permit zone (if any)

5. OPTIONAL: Parking Meter Replacement Program
   If available, locations of newly installed / upgraded meters:
   - Address(es)
   - Installation date
   - New meter type/model
   - Updated rate structure

JUSTIFICATION:
This data will be used to develop a mobile application ("ParkSmart") that provides Los Angeles residents and visitors with clear, accessible parking rule information. The app will:
- Reduce unintentional parking violations
- Improve driver compliance with regulations
- Be provided at no cost to users
- Serve as a public information resource
