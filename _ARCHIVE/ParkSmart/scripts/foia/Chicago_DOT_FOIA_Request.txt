SUBJECT: FOIA Request – Parking Meter Locations, Smart Meters, and Street Parking Rules

This is a request under the Illinois Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) for the following records:

1. SMART METER INVENTORY & RATES
   Complete dataset of all City of Chicago Smart Meter parking meters:
   - Meter ID / asset tag
   - Street location (address, block number)
   - Latitude and longitude coordinates
   - Hourly rate(s) (base rate, time-of-day variations if any)
   - Operating hours (e.g., 8 AM - 10 PM, which days/holidays)
   - Maximum stay duration (in minutes or hours)
   - Zone / district designation
   - Payment methods (credit card, mobile app, etc.)
   - Installation / activation date
   - Current effective date of rates/rules

   Requested format: CSV, Excel (.xlsx), JSON, or GIS shapefile

2. STREET PARKING REGULATIONS (Non-Meter Curb Rules)
   Complete street-by-street parking regulation database:
   - Street name
   - From intersection / from address
   - To intersection / to address
   - Side of street (north, south, east, west)
   - Regulation type (NO PARKING, PARKING 2-4PM, RESIDENT ONLY, etc.)
   - Full regulation text with days and hours
   - Max stay limit (if applicable)
   - Permit zone code (if applicable)
   - Special rules or holidays
   - Effective date of regulation

   Requested format: CSV, Excel, JSON, or GIS-compatible format

3. RESIDENTIAL PERMIT PARKING (RPP) ZONES
   Complete inventory of all Residential Preferential Parking zones:
   - Zone name and code (e.g., Zone A, Zone B, etc.)
   - Street boundaries (from/to streets)
   - Streets included in zone
   - Permit requirements and application process
   - Time restrictions on non-resident parking
   - Current effective boundaries

   Requested format: CSV, Excel, JSON, or GIS shapefile

4. STREET CLEANING / STREET SWEEPING SCHEDULE
   Complete Chicago street cleaning schedule:
   - Street name (or street segment)
   - Cleaning / sweeping day(s) of week (e.g., Monday, Wednesday)
   - Cleaning hours (e.g., 9 AM - 1 PM)
   - Zone / district / ward
   - Current effective schedule

   Requested format: CSV, Excel, JSON, or text table

5. PARKING VIOLATION DATA (OPTIONAL but valuable)
   If available (without personal information):
   - Violation type distribution by street/zone
   - Time-of-day patterns (when violations most frequent)
   - This helps us understand which rules are most critical

   Requested format: Aggregate summary only (no individual ticket data)

JUSTIFICATION:
This data will be used to develop a mobile application ("ParkSmart") that provides Chicago residents and visitors with clear, understandable parking rule information. The app will:
- Help drivers understand complex parking regulations
- Reduce unintentional parking violations
- Be provided free to all users
- Serve as a public information resource benefiting Chicago's parking compliance

PUBLIC BENEFIT / FEE WAIVER REQUEST:
I request a waiver of copying and search fees, as this request is in the public interest. The resulting app will be provided at no cost to users and will reduce parking violations.
